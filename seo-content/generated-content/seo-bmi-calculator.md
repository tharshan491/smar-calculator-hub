## H1: BMI Calculator - Find Your Body Mass Index

### What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a measure of body fat based on height and weight. It provides a simple screening tool for weight categories that may lead to health problems. While BMI doesn't directly measure body fat, medical research shows it's correlated with body fat and related disease risks.

**BMI Formula:**
BMI = Weight (kg) / Height (m)²

Or in imperial units:
BMI = (Weight in pounds × 703) / (Height in inches)²

### BMI Categories Explained

- **Underweight**: BMI less than 18.5
- **Normal Weight**: BMI 18.5 - 24.9
- **Overweight**: BMI 25 - 29.9
- **Obese**: BMI 30 or greater

### Understanding Your BMI Results

**What Your BMI Means**

A BMI result provides a quick assessment of whether your weight falls into a healthy range for your height. However, BMI has limitations:

- Doesn't account for muscle mass (athletes may have high BMI)
- Doesn't distinguish between fat and muscle
- Varies by age, gender, and ethnicity
- Just one screening tool among many

**Additional Health Metrics to Consider:**
- Waist circumference
- Body composition (fat vs. muscle)
- Physical fitness level
- Family health history
- Overall lifestyle habits

### BMI Health Implications

**Underweight (BMI < 18.5)**
- May indicate insufficient nutrition
- Could relate to health conditions
- Recommendation: Consult healthcare provider

**Normal Weight (BMI 18.5-24.9)**
- Generally considered healthy weight range
- Lower risk of weight-related diseases
- Maintain with balanced diet and exercise

**Overweight (BMI 25-29.9)**
- Increased health risks
- May benefit from lifestyle changes
- Good time to start preventive measures

**Obese (BMI ≥ 30)**
- Significantly elevated health risks
- Consider consulting healthcare provider
- Weight management could improve health

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is BMI accurate for athletes and muscular people?**
A: BMI may overestimate body fat in athletes since muscle weighs more than fat. Consider body composition analysis.

**Q: Should I lose weight if my BMI is overweight?**
A: Consult your doctor. Other factors matter: fitness level, age, muscle mass, and overall health.

**Q: How often should I check my BMI?**
A: Monthly checks are reasonable. Weight naturally fluctuates; track trends rather than daily changes.

**Q: What's the difference between adult and child BMI?**
A: Children's BMI uses age-based percentiles. Our calculator has a specific children's BMI feature.

**Q: Can BMI affect insurance premiums?**
A: Some insurers use BMI as one factor, though this is changing as health understanding evolves.
