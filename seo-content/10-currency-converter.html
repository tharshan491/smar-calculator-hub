Understanding Exchange Rates

An exchange rate shows how much of one currency equals another currency. Exchange rates fluctuate constantly based on market conditions. For example: 1 USD = 0.92 EUR (1 US Dollar equals approximately 0.92 Euro).

Why Exchange Rates Change

Exchange rates fluctuate due to various factors:

  • Supply and Demand: More demand for currency increases its value
  • Interest Rates: Higher interest rates attract foreign investment
  • Inflation: Higher inflation generally weakens currency
  • Political Events: Elections, treaties, conflicts affect rates
  • Economic Data: Employment, GDP, trade figures influence rates
  • Market Sentiment: Investor confidence affects currency demand

Understanding Bid-Ask Spread

When converting currency, you'll see two rates:

  • Bid Price: What the dealer will pay for the currency
  • Ask Price: What the dealer will charge to sell the currency
  • Spread: The difference between bid and ask (dealer's profit)

Always get the best possible spread when converting large amounts.

Currency Converter FAQs

Why do exchange rates differ from spot rates?

Banks and services add markups to spot rates. They charge fees, commissions, and spreads to profit from transactions.

Are online currency converters accurate?

Yes, online converters use real-time rates but may lag by a few seconds. For actual transactions, confirm with your bank or exchange service.

Can I make money from currency conversion?

Some do through forex trading, but it's risky and requires significant knowledge. For personal use, focus on getting best rates rather than profit.

How often do exchange rates update?

Forex rates update in real-time during market hours. Our converter updates frequently to reflect current rates.

Is currency conversion free?

The calculator is free. However, actual currency exchange involves fees from banks or exchange services. Compare options for best rates.
