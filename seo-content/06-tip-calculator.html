Understanding Tips and Gratuity

A tip is a voluntary payment given to service workers as appreciation for their work. In the US, tipping is customary for restaurants, taxis, hotels, and many services. Tips typically calculate as a percentage of the bill before tax.

Standard Tip Percentages by Service

  • Restaurant (table service): 15-20% is standard, 18% average
  • Takeout/Counter service: 10-15% or $1-2 minimum
  • Delivery services: 10-15% or $3-5 minimum
  • Barista/Coffee shops: $1-2 or 15%
  • Hair stylist/Barber: 15-20%
  • Spa/Massage: 15-20%
  • Taxi/Ride sharing: 15-20%
  • Bellhop/Valet: $2-5 per service
  • Housekeeping: $2-5 per room per night

How to Calculate Tips Manually

The tip formula: Tip Amount = Bill Amount × (Tip Percentage / 100)

Example: $50 bill with 20% tip = $50 × 0.20 = $10 tip

Total bill = $50 + $10 = $60

When to Adjust Tip Amount

  • Exceptional Service: Consider 20-25% for outstanding service
  • Poor Service: 10-15% is acceptable for mediocre service
  • Very Bad Service: Can give 5-8% or skip tip if truly awful
  • Large Groups: Check if gratuity is automatically added (often 18-20%)
  • Cash Shortages: Round up what you can afford
  • Budget-Conscious: Baseline tip funds service workers' wages

Tip vs Service Charge - What's the Difference?

  • Tip: Optional, customer decides amount, goes to individual servers
  • Service Charge: Automatic fee added to bill, may be shared among staff

Always ask if service charge is included before tipping the standard percentage on top.

Tips for Different Payment Methods

  • Credit Card: Easy to add tip amount when signing receipt
  • Debit Card: Add tip at terminal or on receipt
  • Cash Payment: Leave physical cash or coins on table
  • Mobile Payment: Apps like Venmo allow tip options
  • Online Delivery: Tip is added during checkout or after delivery

Splitting Bill and Tip Between Friends

When splitting with friends, divide both bill and tip equally:

  • Calculate total bill (including tip)
  • Divide total by number of people
  • Each person pays their share

Example: $120 bill + $24 tip = $144 total ÷ 4 people = $36 per person

Regional Tipping Customs

Tipping customs vary by country:

  • USA: 15-20% expected for table service
  • Canada: 15-18% standard, similar to US
  • Europe: Service included in menu price; round up or 5-10%
  • UK: 10-15% optional if service wasn't included
  • Australia: No obligation, 5-10% for exceptional service
  • Japan: Tipping is not customary and may be refused

Tip Calculator FAQs

Should I calculate tip on pre-tax or post-tax bill?

Standard practice is to calculate on the pre-tax bill amount. However, some people tip on post-tax bill, which results in about 8% higher tip.

What if I can't afford a 20% tip?

Give what you can afford. Any tip is appreciated. At minimum, try 10-15%, or a few dollars even if percentage seems low.

Should I tip delivery drivers?

Yes, delivery drivers typically earn less. 10-15% or $3-5 minimum is appropriate, especially for bad weather or far distances.

Is it rude not to tip?

In countries where tipping is customary (like USA), not tipping is considered rude. Workers depend on tips for income.

Can I adjust tip after paying?

With credit cards, you can sometimes adjust tip when signing receipt. For cash, you cannot adjust after leaving. Plan ahead with our calculator.
