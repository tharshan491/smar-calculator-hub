What is a Percentage?

A percentage represents a fraction of 100. The symbol % means "per hundred." Percentages help express quantities relative to a whole. For example, 50% means 50 out of 100, or half of something.

Percentage Formula Explained

The basic percentage formula is simple: (Part / Whole) × 100 = Percentage

Example: If you scored 85 out of 100 on a test, your percentage = (85/100) × 100 = 85%

To find a percentage of a number: Number × (Percentage/100) = Result

Example: 20% of 250 = 250 × (20/100) = 50

Percentage Increase vs Percentage Decrease

These are different calculations:

  • Percentage Increase: (New Value - Old Value) / Old Value × 100
  • Percentage Decrease: (Old Value - New Value) / Old Value × 100

Example: If stock price goes from $100 to $120, increase = (120-100)/100 × 100 = 20% increase

Percentage Calculator FAQs

What's the difference between percentage and percentile?

Percentage is a fraction of 100. Percentile is the position in a ranking (like test scores where 90th percentile means better than 90% of test-takers).

How do I calculate percentage of a percentage?

Multiply the percentages together. For example, 50% of 50% = 0.5 × 0.5 = 0.25 = 25%.

Can percentages be more than 100%?

Yes! A 150% increase means something doubled plus 50% more. This is common with growth metrics.

How do I work backwards from a percentage?

If something is 75% of a value and equals 300, the original = 300 ÷ 0.75 = 400.

Are percentage calculations the same worldwide?

Yes, percentage calculations are universal. The % symbol means 1/100 everywhere in the world.
