## H1: Compound Interest Calculator - Watch Your Money Grow

### What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is the interest earned on both your principal investment AND the accumulated interest from previous periods. Often called "interest on interest," it's one of the most powerful forces in wealth building.

Albert Einstein famously called it "the eighth wonder of the world" because of its exponential growth potential.

**The Power of Compounding:**
$10,000 at 10% annual interest:
- Year 1: $11,000
- Year 5: $16,105
- Year 10: $25,937
- Year 20: $67,275

Notice how the growth accelerates over time? That's the magic of compounding.

### How Compound Interest Works

**Formula:**
A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:
- A = Final amount
- P = Principal (initial investment)
- r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
- n = Number of times interest compounds per year
- t = Time in years

**Compounding Frequencies:**
- Annual: Once per year
- Semi-annual: Twice per year
- Quarterly: 4 times per year
- Monthly: 12 times per year
- Daily: 365 times per year
- Continuous: Infinitely often

The more frequently interest compounds, the more you earn.

### Understanding Your Results

**Principal Amount**
Your initial investment. This remains unchanged in calculations.

**Total Interest Earned**
The pure profit from compound growth. This increases exponentially over time.

**Final Amount**
Principal + all compounded interest. This is what your investment grows to.

**Interest vs. Principal Ratio**
In early years, mostly principal; in later years, mostly interest. This shift accelerates over time.

### Key Factors Affecting Compound Interest

**1. Principal Amount**
Doubling your initial investment doubles your earnings.

**2. Interest Rate**
Higher rates have exponential effect. 10% vs 5% can double your money in half the time.

**3. Time Period**
Time is your greatest asset. Starting early matters tremendously:
- Invest $5,000 at age 25 (40 years, 8%): $215,000+
- Invest $5,000 at age 35 (30 years, 8%): $100,000+
- Invest $5,000 at age 45 (20 years, 8%): $46,600+

**4. Compounding Frequency**
Daily compounding yields slightly more than monthly, which yields more than annual.

**5. Regular Contributions**
Adding $100/month significantly increases final amount over 10-20+ years.

### Compound Interest FAQs

**Q: Where can I get these interest rates?**
A: High-yield savings accounts (4-5%), CDs (4-5%), regular bonds (3-4%), stocks (10% average), etc.

**Q: Is compound interest guaranteed?**
A: Stock market returns vary yearly. Savings accounts and CDs are safer but offer lower rates.

**Q: How often should I compound?**
A: For savings accounts, monthly or daily is typical. For investments, annual is common but varies.

**Q: Can I compound manually?**
A: Yes, but automatic compounding is easier. Most banks do this automatically.

**Q: What's the Rule of 72?**
A: Divide 72 by interest rate to estimate years to double. 72÷8% = 9 years to double at 8% interest.

### Tips for Maximizing Compound Interest

1. **Start Early**: Time is more important than amount
2. **Be Consistent**: Regular contributions compound too
3. **Reinvest Earnings**: Don't withdraw interest to maximize compounding
4. **Look for Higher Rates**: 1% difference compounds significantly over decades
5. **Reduce Taxes**: Use tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA, HSA)
6. **Minimize Fees**: High fees eat into returns
7. **Stay Invested**: Resist selling during market downturns
8. **Diversify**: Spread risk across multiple investments
