What is Net Salary?

Net salary is your actual take-home pay after all deductions. It's different from gross salary, which is the total before taxes and other deductions. Our calculator shows you both amounts clearly.

Understanding Salary Components

Your salary consists of several components:

  • Base Salary: Fixed monthly or annual compensation
  • Bonus: Additional compensation based on performance
  • Allowances: HRA (housing), conveyance, medical, dearness, etc.
  • Gross Salary: Sum of all above components

Common Salary Deductions

  • Income Tax (IT): Progressive tax based on salary bracket
  • Professional Tax: State-based deduction
  • Employee PF (Provident Fund): Typically 12% of basic salary
  • Health Insurance: Insurance premium deductions
  • Loan EMI: If employee has company loan
  • Leave Travel Allowance (LTA): Some companies deduct advance
  • Advance Salary: If taken, deducted in installments

Gross Salary vs Net Salary

  • Gross Salary: Total earning before any deductions
  • Net Salary: Amount received after all deductions

Example: If gross is $3,000 and deductions are $600, net salary is $2,400

Tax Brackets and How They Work

Income tax is typically calculated using progressive tax brackets:

  • Slab 1: 0-$10,000: 0% tax
  • Slab 2: $10,001-$40,000: 10% tax on amount in this bracket
  • Slab 3: $40,001-$85,000: 20% tax on amount in this bracket
  • Slab 4: Above $85,000: 30% tax on amount in this bracket

Important: Tax is calculated per bracket, not on entire salary!

Employee vs Employer Benefits

Some benefits reduce salary deductions:

  • Company Health Insurance: Employer pays, reduces employee deduction
  • Travel Allowance: Tax-free up to limit
  • Food Vouchers: Benefit reduces taxable salary
  • Stock Options: May have tax implications

Different Salary Structures

  • Fixed Salary: Same every month
  • Variable Salary: Changes based on performance/sales
  • Part Salary + Bonus: Base salary plus performance bonus
  • Commission Only: Entirely based on sales
  • Hourly Wage: Paid based on hours worked

Annual vs Monthly Salary Calculations

  • Annual Calculation: Total for entire year at once
  • Monthly Calculation: Annual divided by 12

Note: Progressive tax brackets use annual income, so always verify annualized calculation.

Salary Calculator FAQs

Why is net salary less than half of gross?

Multiple deductions combine: income tax (10-30%), PF (12%), professional tax (1-2%), health insurance (2-3%), and other deductions add up significantly.

What are tax slabs?

Tax brackets where progressive rates apply. Lower earnings are taxed lower, higher earnings taxed higher. Calculated on brackets, not entire salary.

Can I reduce my tax burden?

Yes, through investments in retirement accounts (401k, IRA), tax-saving schemes (life insurance, medical insurance), and claiming deductions.

How does PF (Provident Fund) work?

PF is a retirement savings scheme. Employee contributes 12% of basic salary, employer contributes 12% (not deducted from salary). You can withdraw at retirement or specific conditions.

Is HRA (House Rent Allowance) needed even if I don't rent?

HRA is a component of salary whether you rent or not. It provides tax benefits under Section 10(13A). You can claim exemption even without renting in some cases.
