What is TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure)?

TDEE is the total number of calories your body burns in a day including all activities and metabolism. It consists of BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) plus calories burned through activities. Our calculator provides your personalized TDEE.

Understanding BMR and TDEE

  • BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate): Calories burned at rest for basic body functions
  • Activity Calories: Extra calories burned through exercise and daily activities
  • TDEE: Total of BMR plus activity calories = total calorie burn per day

Activity Levels Explained

  • Sedentary: Little or no exercise, mostly desk job = BMR × 1.2
  • Lightly Active: Light exercise 1-3 days/week = BMR × 1.375
  • Moderately Active: Exercise 3-5 days/week = BMR × 1.55
  • Very Active: Intense exercise 6-7 days/week = BMR × 1.725
  • Extremely Active: Athletes and very intense training = BMR × 1.9

Using TDEE for Your Goals

Once you know your TDEE, you can adjust calorie intake based on goals:

  • Weight Loss: Eat 300-500 calories below TDEE (0.5-1 lb per week loss)
  • Weight Gain/Muscle: Eat 300-500 calories above TDEE
  • Maintenance: Eat at TDEE level to maintain current weight
  • Gradual Changes: Smaller deficits are sustainable long-term

Factors Affecting Your TDEE

  • Age: Metabolism decreases with age (~2% per decade after 30)
  • Gender: Men typically have higher TDEE than women
  • Height and Weight: Larger bodies burn more calories
  • Muscle Mass: Muscle burns more calories than fat
  • Activity Level: Major factor in total calorie burn
  • Genetics: Some individuals have naturally faster/slower metabolism

Metabolism and Calorie Burning

Your body burns calories through three main mechanisms:

  • Basal Metabolism: 60-75% of TDEE - basic body functions
  • Thermic Effect: 10% of TDEE - digestion and processing food
  • Activity: 15-30% of TDEE - exercise and movement

Calorie Myths Debunked

  • Myth: All calories are equal - False: Your body processes 100 cal of protein differently than 100 cal of sugar
  • Myth: Low calorie always works - False: Extreme deficits cause muscle loss and metabolic slowdown
  • Myth: Exercise overrides calories - False: Diet is primary factor; exercise aids but can't outrun bad diet
  • Myth: TDEE is fixed - False: It changes with weight, muscle mass, and activity

Nutrition Tips with Calorie Awareness

  • Macronutrient Balance: Balance proteins, carbs, and fats within calorie goal
  • Protein Intake: 0.7-1g per pound of bodyweight supports muscle
  • Whole Foods: Focus on nutrient-dense foods, not just calorie count
  • Hydration: Water has zero calories but aids satiety and metabolism
  • Fiber: High-fiber foods provide satiety with fewer calories
  • Sustainable Deficit: 500 calorie deficit = 1 lb/week loss, sustainable long-term

Tracking Calories - Best Practices

  • Use food tracking apps for accurate calorie logging
  • Weigh food portions for accuracy, especially in beginning
  • Include condiments and cooking oils (they have calories!)
  • Track beverages including coffee, juice, alcohol
  • Be honest about portion sizes
  • Track consistently for results

Calorie Calculator FAQs

Is TDEE the same for everyone with my stats?

No, individual metabolism varies. These are estimates. Track results for 2-3 weeks and adjust based on actual weight changes.

Should I eat back exercise calories?

Partially. Don't eat back 100% as estimates are often high. Eat back 50-75% of logged calories for safety.

What's the minimum calories I should eat?

Never go below 1,200-1,500 calories (consult doctor). Extreme restriction causes muscle loss, fatigue, and metabolic damage.

How long until I see weight loss results?

With proper 500 calorie deficit, expect 0.5-1 lb loss per week. Give it 4 weeks before adjusting.

Does metabolism slow with age?

Yes, roughly 2% decrease per decade after 30. Strength training can maintain muscle and metabolism.
